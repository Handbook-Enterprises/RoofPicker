Common questions
About black roofs.

The questions Google's "People Also Ask" surfaces for this query, answered without sales spin.

Black is the most popular pairing, with charcoal a close second. The high contrast between a white siding and a near-black roof reads "modern American classic" and works on every architectural style from cottage to colonial. Avoid stark cool whites under a black roof — slightly warm whites like Benjamin Moore Simply White or Sherwin-Williams Alabaster pair best.

Modestly. A black roof adds about 5 to 10°F to attic temperatures during peak Florida summer compared to a light gray roof. With modern attic ventilation and a radiant barrier, the actual impact on monthly cooling costs is typically under $20. Most homeowners find the aesthetic worth the trade-off.

Not because of the color. Florida insurers care about wind rating, hail rating, and roof age. Every black-roof SKU on this page is 130 mph wind and Class 3 hail or better. For impact discounts, look for Class 4 (hail-rated) variants — available across most of the recommended brands.

By install volume: GAF Timberline HDZ Charcoal. By design-magazine appearances: Owens Corning Duration Onyx Black. By Florida hurricane-zone preference: CertainTeed Landmark Pro Moire Black. All three are within a few dollars per square of each other at retail.

Asphalt architectural: 25 to 35 years with proper installation. Metal standing seam: 50 to 70 years. Color holds through most of that span thanks to ceramic-coated granules (asphalt) or factory-baked Kynar finish (metal).

Algae streaks are more visible on light-colored roofs (which they appear gray-black against). On a black roof, the streaks blend in and are essentially invisible. Algae warranty terms still apply but it is rare to ever notice the streaks.
