The color · in plain words
What you're looking at.

Charcoal is the workhorse dark gray of the asphalt shingle category. Not quite black, not quite slate. It's a confident, anchored color that absorbs the eye instead of bouncing it. On a south-facing Florida elevation it reads cool and slightly blue. On a north-facing roof in shade it pulls toward true black. That tonal range is the reason architects keep specifying it on contemporary builds.

The granule blend uses a base of deep ink-blue with charcoal-gray accents and a faint warm undertone to keep the surface from going dead-flat. In direct midday sun the texture catches enough light to show the dimensional shadow line that gives Timberline HDZ its premium feel. At dusk the whole roof becomes one quiet plane that frames the rest of the house instead of competing with it.

Pairs cleanly with white, cream, navy, sage, and warm terra-cotta sidings. Almost any house color works underneath it, which is why it accounts for roughly a third of all Timberline HDZ installs nationally. If you want a roof that disappears into "looks good," this is the safe bet.
