The decision · plainly
Two good shingles. One right for you.
Subject A

When to choose Owens Corning Duration.

  • You want a longer manufacturer warranty (50 years vs 30 years material defect).
  • SureNail strip is the install confidence-builder your contractor swears by.
  • You prefer the broader range of muted, earthy colors (think Driftwood, Sand Castle).
  • Algae warranty is 10 years and that matters to you.
  • Owens Corning has stronger Lowe's distribution if you are sourcing materials yourself.
Subject B

When to choose GAF Timberline HDZ.

  • You want the deepest color library on the market (33 colors vs 19).
  • Your contractor is GAF-certified (Master Elite or higher) for extended warranty access.
  • StrikeZone nailing area means faster installs and fewer crew errors.
  • You want GAF's WindProven limited wind warranty with no maximum wind speed.
  • Home Depot is closer than Lowe's and you are sourcing locally.

Comparison FAQ · plainly answered
Common questions.

The questions People Also Ask on Google for this comparison, answered without sales spin.

On paper, Owens Corning Duration has a longer material warranty (50 years vs 30 years). In practice, both shingles routinely last 25 to 35 years when installed correctly with proper ventilation. Warranty length matters less than install quality.

Both are rated 130 mph. GAF Timberline HDZ has the WindProven limited warranty with no maximum wind speed, which is the strongest wind warranty in the asphalt category. Florida installers tend to slightly prefer HDZ for hurricane zones because of this.

Within about $8 per square at retail. On a typical 30-square home that is roughly $240 difference in materials. Local supply availability often matters more than the brand-level retail spread.

Technically yes, but you would lose both manufacturer warranties because each requires complete-system installation of accessories from the same brand. Stick with one.

GAF Timberline HDZ has 33 colors including regional exclusives. Owens Corning Duration has 19 colors on the TruDefinition platform. If color choice matters most to you, HDZ wins.

Most Florida insurers care about the wind rating and hail rating, not the brand. Both are Class 3 hail and 130 mph wind, so insurance pricing should be similar. Ask your insurer specifically about WindProven coverage if you choose HDZ.
